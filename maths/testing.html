Disease Testing

Notes:

  • The probability that you have the disease given a positive test result is calculated by $$P(D+|T+)=\frac{\text{False Positives}}{\text{Total Positives}}=\frac{ap}{ap+(1-a)(1-p)}$$ where $a$ is the accuracy, and $p$ the prevalence.
  • In reality, the concept of accuracy is split into sensitivity, the proportion of the positive test results which correctly identify the disease, and specificity the proportion of negative test results that correctly identify the absence of the disease. The simplification used here assumes they are the same, but in general this is not the case!
